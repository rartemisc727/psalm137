Psalm 137

"By the rivers of Babylon..."

☪ ✝ ✡
Home Context Analysis

Psalm 137 is set during the Babylonian Exile following the destruction of the First Temple in 586 BCE. Many Judeans were forcibly taken to Babylon, where they experienced displacement and cultural separation from Jerusalem.

Timeline of Events

c.1000 BCE - United Monarchy

King David establishes Jerusalem as a political and religious center in Israelite tradition.

960 BCE - First Temple Built

Solomon constructs the First Temple in Jerusalem, centralizing worship.

722 BCE - Assyrian Conquest

Northern Kingdom of Israel falls to Assyria, leading to earlier waves of exile.

586 BCE - Babylonian Destruction of Jerusalem

The Babylonians destroy Jerusalem and the First Temple. Mass deportation to Babylon begins.

586-539 BCE - Babylonian Exile

Exiled Judeans live in Babylon. Psalm 137 is written during this period of displacement and mourning.

539 BCE - Persian Conquest of Babylon

Persian King Cyrus the Great conquers Babylon and allows exiled peoples, including the Jews, to return home

538 BCE - Return to Jerusalem

Some exiles return and begin rebuilding Jerusalem and the Temple (Second Temple period begins.)

Tisha B'Av

  • 9th of Av
  • Day of fasting
  • Commemorates the destruction of the First Temple (586 BCE) and the Second Temple (70 CE)
  • Observed as a day of grief for major historical tragedies in Jewish history
  • Centers on themes of loss, exile, and the destruction of Jerusalem
  • Traditional to refrain from public celebrations, washing, anointing with oils, shaving, sexual activity, and wearing leather
